BSc (Hons) Information Technology

About Information Technology(IT)

Information Technology(IT) is the practice of storing, processing, retrieving, and transmitting data and information using computer hardware, software, and telecommunications tools. It includes a broad range of applications and technology, such as computers, networks, databases, internet, mobile devices, and more.IT specialists use these technologies to build, administer, and maintain the information systems that help businesses, organisations, and governments run their operations. They create and develop software programmes, set up and maintain computer networks and servers, safeguard data systems from online attacks, and assist end users with technical issues.

Programme Overview

With the help of this programme, you will gain the technical know-how you need to stay up with the quick changes in technology and to hone your analytical abilities. Through the carefully constructed coursework and industrial training, students enrolled in this programme are exposed to both technical and practical experiences relevant to the real world, preparing them to enter the workforce after graduation. Fast-moving technology-based industries need workers with a balanced understanding of both physical infrastructure and software development. You will be skilled at creating an organization's technological infrastructure and providing its users with support if you combine your technical IT knowledge with practical experience. This flexible curriculum is best suited for people who want to advance as computer generalists and establish lucrative careers as IT specialists and managers. You can explore the different electives that are available and further your knowledge in specialised areas like network administration and security, database engineering, and mobile development based on your unique interests.

Entry Requirement

STPM: Average C+ or CGPA 2.33 (minimum 2 Principals)
A-Level: Minimum 12 points (minimum Grade B in at least 2 subjects)
Australian Matriculation: ATAR 60
Canadian Matriculation: 60%
Unified Examination Certificate: Maximum 26 points from 5 subjects (all Grade Bs including Mathematics)
International Baccalaureate: Completed with minimum 25 points (excluding bonus points)
Other Qualifications: Any other equivalent qualifications. An applicant with no standard qualification will be considered on a case-to-case basis

Specific Requirement

Credit in Additional Mathematics at SPM or its equivalent Graduates from Foundation/Matriculation programmes without a credit in Additional Mathematics subject at SPM level to enrol and Pass a Mathematics Enhancement subject offered by the programme; and credit in Mathematics and one subject from either Science, Technology or Engineering discipline at SPM level.

The International Preparatory Programme (IPP) is an intensive six-month preparatory programme. International students or candidates from certain countries that want to take Harvord University’s certain degree programmes, they will need to take and pass our online six-month International Preparatory Programme (IPP) to be eligible to enrol on a Harvord degree programme that also leads to a degree certificate awarded by Harvord’s partner institution. Students are required to pass all the subjects with C and above to qualify for this.
